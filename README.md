The **National Outcomes Marketplace (NOM)** is a revolutionary government-run digital platform that transforms how India delivers public services. Instead of paying for activities (trainings conducted, toilets built, devices distributed), governments pay only for **verified outcomes** (jobs sustained, toilets used, learning improved).

This is outcome-based governance at national scale, powered by Digital India infrastructure.

## ğŸ”„ How It Works

### Step A: Government Posts Outcome Contracts
Ministries, states, or districts post contracts like:
- Reduce dropout rate in Block X by Y%
- Ensure 85% TB treatment completion in District Y
- Increase groundwater level by Z centimeters
- Achieve 70% household waste segregation in Ward Z
- Improve foundational literacy by X points

**Each contract includes:**
- Target outcome metric
- Eligible interventions (optional)
- Time window
- Payment per verified result
- Standardized measurement method

### Step B: Implementers Bid and Deliver
Implementers (startups, NGOs, SHGs, FPOs, universities, citizen consortia) bid with:
- Their approach
- Cost per outcome unit
- Proof of capacity

### Step C: Verification Through Outcome Proof Rails
**Key Innovation:** Standardized verification using:
- Existing government datasets
- Third-party audits
- Sample surveys
- Geo-tagged proof
- Time-stamped media proofs
- Tamper-resistant logs

**Core Principle:** Outcome evidence must be auditable and reproducible.

### Step D: Payments Triggered Only for Outcomes
Results = Payment. No results = No payment.

**Benefits:**
- Immediately reduces waste
- Rewards innovation
- Exposes ineffective programs quickly
- Enables cross-district/state comparison
